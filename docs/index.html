floodwall

A zero-dependency Rust crate that meters agent-generated infrastructure changes, verifies each intent at a policy gate, and records every verdict in a tamper-evident ledger.

The wall

Agents submit intents. Production gets decisions.

01

Admission

A per-agent token bucket and bounded priority queue keep one runaway loop from starving the rest of the fleet.

02

Gate

A deny-overrides policy stack evaluates every intent, so one hard rejection blocks the change.

03

Ledger

Every admit, defer, and reject lands in an append-only hash chain that fails verification if history is edited.

Crate surface

Small enough to read. Structured enough to extend.

floodwall models a change as an attributed intent with an action, priority, and blast radius. The caller supplies logical time, so admission control stays deterministic and testable.

Intent
Apply, scale, or destroy a resource with explicit blast radius.
Policy
Pure Rust trait returning admit, defer, or reject.
Floodwall
End-to-end submit, gate, and record flow.
use floodwall::{Admission, Floodwall, Gate, RateLimit};
use floodwall::policy::{BlastNeedsPriority, NoGlobalDestroy};

let admission = Admission::new(1024, RateLimit::new(8.0, 1.0));
let gate = Gate::new()
    .with(NoGlobalDestroy)
    .with(BlastNeedsPriority);

let mut plane = Floodwall::new(admission, gate);
let decision = plane.tick();

assert!(plane.ledger().verify());

Roadmap

From wall to scheduler to replayable control plane.

v0.1

The wall

Intent model, admission control, policy gate, hash-chained ledger, demo binary.

v0.2

Scheduler

Serialize wide-blast and same-resource intents while narrow independent work proceeds.

v0.3

Trustworthy ledger

SHA-256 chain, signed records, and Merkle checkpoints for suffix audits.

v0.4

Persistence and replay

Durable append-only state and replay that reconstructs the live decisions.
